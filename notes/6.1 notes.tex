6.1 Exponential Growth and Decay

1. Growth vs Decay
base > 1 = growth, 0 < base < 1 = decay
$$ y = 2^x \text{ (growth)} \qquad y = \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^x \text{ (decay)} $$

2. Exponential Models
a = initial amount, r = percent change (decimal)
$$ \text{Growth: } y = a(1+r)^t $$
$$ \text{Decay: } y = a(1-r)^t $$

3. Car Value Example
$$ y = 25(0.85)^t $$
decay factor 0.85, so r = 1 - 0.85 = 0.15 = 15% decrease/yr
y = 8 when t = 7 (use calculator)

4. World Population Example
a = 7.38B, r = 1.09% = 0.0109
$$ y = 7.38(1.0109)^t $$
t = 5 (year 2020): y = 7.79 billion

5. Rewriting Exponential Functions
Find daily decay rate from $y = a(0.5)^{t/28}$
$$ (0.5)^{1/28} = 0.9755 = 1 - 0.0245 $$
daily decay rate = 2.45%

6. Compound Interest
P = principal, r = annual rate, n = times/yr, t = years
$$ A = P\left(1 + \frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt} $$
$9000 at 1.46%, quarterly (n=4), t=3:
$$ A = 9000\left(1 + \frac{0.0146}{4}\right)^{4 \cdot 3} \approx 9402.21 $$
